Who We Are

The problem with plastics is getting worse each day. There may be around 5.25 trillion macro and microplastic pieces floating in the open ocean. Plastics, consistently make up 60% to 90% of all marine debris according to studies. Plastics are everywhere. It is very powerful, light, cheap and versatile. Some plastic wastes are not reused or recycled, and experts believe that 50% of plastics are single-use, which means it is used once before discarding. Single-use plastics include plastic bottles, plastic packaging, and plastic grocery bags.

Most plastic forms are technically recyclable, but most municipal governments do not have the infrastructure in place for this fuel-intensive operation to be carried out. Recycling plastic does not only require large amount of energy but also uses large quantities of water. With these mentioned studies, the researchers came up with an idea that aims to contribute to the elimination or at least to help minimize the number of plastic wastes.

Most single-used plastic wastes are coming from peopleâ€™s basic needs like water and food that people buy in stores or shops like coffee shop, milk tea shop, convenience stores, groceries and malls. Project: ZeroW aims to target these kinds of businesses to make a great impact in minimizing the number of plastic wastes. But it is not the only solution to the plastic wastes problem, it still depends on the discipline and participation of the people.

Project: ZeroW is a system that maybe used by stores like coffee shops, milk tea shops, fast food, restaurants, convenience store, and even groceries. It will integrate biometric system to access the user account. For every use of reusable plastic container for their shopping or purchase, they will be provided reward points that they may use to redeem items from any of the partner stores. They may also use their points to purchase from these stores.
